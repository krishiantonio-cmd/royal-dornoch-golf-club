Where general policies quietly leave gaps.

A standard commercial policy covers the clubhouse and basics, and stops there. For a club of Royal Dornoch's scale, two courses, a new £13.9 million clubhouse, £1.5 million irrigation infrastructure, newly acquired land, event hosting at championship level, and the 2028 Curtis Cup on the horizon, the gaps between a general policy and a specialist one are material. Here is what that difference looks like, side by side.

What standard cover often misses

  • New clubhouse reinstatement at current build cost (£13.9M replacement value)
  • Greenkeeper plant and irrigation infrastructure, correctly valued
  • Play cover for members and competition across both courses
  • Visiting society and event liability at championship scale
  • Event cancellation for major amateur championships and the 2028 Curtis Cup
  • Newly acquired land and future course development during construction phase

What a bespoke golf scheme covers

  • Full public liability including events, visiting societies and championship-scale gatherings
  • Both Championship and Struie course infrastructure, correctly scheduled
  • Play cover for members and competition across all courses
  • Greenkeeper personal accident and plant cover, valued at current replacement cost
  • Event cancellation built for clubs hosting national and international fixtures
  • Comprehensive policy wording, built for golf, not adapted from commercial templates
